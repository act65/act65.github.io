Story / alternative history.

What if mathematics followed different norms?
What if a famous mathematician (idk, Gauss, Fourier, ... who would be best to start the trend?) decided; instead of publishing my ideas, I'll offer them as a service (solve problem X for $$).

(I imagine a solve via mail service. People would send their industry relevant math problems to A, they solve the problem and send back the results)

What would happen? What would this world look like? How would it progress?

- slower development of math, which leads to slower progress in science, economics, computers, EVERYTHING!!
- the largest companies are math companies!?
- sometimes an amature / rebel solves one of these math problems and publishes it (for free). Very rare - effectively giving away money...
- intellectual property for math?
- ???

Part of the point of this story is to highlight two things;

- that mathematics has a MASSIVE contribution to progress and welfare, (In my view, this is very underappreciated)
- that mathematicians are not rewarded proportional to their contributions. (pushing back on the capitalist narrative that people are rewarded for the utility they bring to others)
